Overview

SynDex is a surgical training simulator designed to replicate real surgical robotics and procedures. We found an issue in the training of future medical professionals: there is a lack of realistic, hands-on training opportunities for surgeons and medical students to practice minimally invasive surgical techniques. We set out to fix this issue. After a year of development, we have created the first version of SynDex, which includes a realistic feel for using a manipulator by completing games and tasks. While SynDex was a phenominal first step, we have plans to continue development to include two arms, better haptic feedback, and more realistic simulations. I am so excited to be leading SynDexV2 this year, and I am looking forward to learning more about medical technology from a leadership standpoint.

My Role

I contributed to the development of SynDex through my work on the firmware team. I worked with ODrives encoders, and led a lot of the research and development for admittance control. For SynDexV2, I am leading the firmware and software teams. I plan to take a strong lead on the haptic feedback, and all improvements to the training software. I also get to help lead the team, which is a new and exciting experience for me.

Technical Details

  • Tools: Real-time simulation software, robotics APIs, 3D graphics engines, haptic feedback systems
  • Concepts: Robotic control, haptic feedback, real-time physics simulation, surgical procedure modeling
  • Impact: Enables surgeons to train on complex procedures with realistic feedback and instrument control
